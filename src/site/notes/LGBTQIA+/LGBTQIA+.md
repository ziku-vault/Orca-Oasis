Typically, LGBTQ+ and LGBTQIA+ are the widely used terms to reference the community of queer people. Their structure is an initialism for lesbian (L), gay (G), bisexual (B), transgender (T), queer (Q), intersex (I) and asexual (A). 

Originally, queer people were often referred to as just 'homosexuals'—coined in 1868 by an Austrian Hungarian, Karl Maria. In addition, Karl Maria invented the term heterosexual, which both of these terms are still being used in modern biology.
